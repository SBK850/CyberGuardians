Cyberbullying: What Is It and How Do We Prevent It?

Cyberbullying is nowadays a real problem that affects boys, girls, and young people around the globe. But despite its increased presence in newspapers, online news, and even in parent-teacher meetings, there remains a great deal of ignorance about what cyberbullying is or how to prevent it.

What Is Cyberbullying?

The definition of cyberbullying indicates that it is harassment carried out between users of a similar age and equivalent social context, through the use of digital media, from mobile phones to the Internet or via online video games, to name a few examples.

Types and Characteristics of Cyberbullying

Indeed, there are many types of cyberbullying. These include harassment, sexting, and stalking. Cyberbullying tends to occur among users of a similar age and social context, using digital means without adult intervention. The objective is often psychological harassment.

Causes of Cyberbullying

Key causes include widespread use of social media among children, anonymity on the Internet, and the harasser's values and education. Younger users' lack of information also exposes them to risks affecting their privacy and intimacy.

Understanding Cyberbullying from a Psychological Perspective

Most cyberbullying cases feature anonymity, a lack of perception of the harm caused, and the adoption of imaginary digital roles. This type of harassment can extend from digital spaces to personal contexts, such as schools and neighborhoods.

How to Prevent Cyberbullying

  • Teach them the importance of keeping their privacy protected and never sharing personal information.
  • Educate them and set an example regarding the content that is shared on the Internet.
  • Ensure that the child's contacts are friends and known persons. Just as they should not speak to strangers in real life, they should not do so online either.
  • Make it clear that you offer your support in any situation of cyberbullying or digital harassment, because they have done nothing wrong: they are the victims.
  • If necessary, take advantage of the existing legal channels to report a situation of cyberbullying.
