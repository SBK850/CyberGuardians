CyberGuardians: Combating Cyberbullying

My name is Seikou Bah Karagera, a third-year Computer Science student at Manchester Metropolitan University. For my thesis, I have developed CyberGuardians, a platform dedicated to combating cyberbullying, an increasing concern that significantly affects the mental health of young people in the UK and globally.

CyberGuardians utilises advanced algorithms and machine learning to detect toxic and harmful content on social media in real-time. This innovative technology is supported by tools such as Google's Perspective API, which analyses online content and detects indicators of cyber harassment in milliseconds. This immediate response capability is crucial in mitigating the impact of cyberbullying before it can cause harm.

Statistics indicate that many young people do not report cyberbullying due to a lack of accessible tools or because they do not consider it serious enough. This platform not only provides a technological solution for identifying and removing harmful content but also creates a safe and welcoming environment for young people, helping them navigate a safer digital space.

Furthermore, CyberGuardians plays a key role in educating and empowering the community to recognise and respond to cyberbullying. It provides resources and support for both victims and perpetrators, promoting a community approach to addressing and preventing online harassment. This reflects the importance of professional intervention and emotional and psychological support in managing these challenges, as highlighted by Dennis Relojo-Howell.

In summary, CyberGuardians represents a collaborative and technologically advanced effort to ensure that digital environments are spaces for positive and safe interaction, significantly reducing the harmful effects of cyberbullying in our society.

By. Seikou Bah Karagera.
